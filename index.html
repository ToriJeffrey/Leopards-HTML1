Leopards

Overview

  • Scientific name: Panthera pardus
  • Type: Mammal
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 92-190 cm
  • Weight: 66-176 pounds
  • Life span: 10-12 years in the wild
  • Vulnerability: Vulnerable

Appearance

The leopard is a middle-sized cat which has a slim and long body, with strong, sturdy legs and a long tail. They have a light tan body with black spots known as rosettes as they are similar to the appearance of roses.

Habitat

Leopards live in Africa, West Asia, Siberia and other areas. They can be found in a wide range of habitats, althoug they usually prefer being in areas covered by trees.

Diet and hunting

Leopards are carnivores; animals which eat meat. They prey on a wide range of creatures including: gazelles, antelopes, jackals, sheep, rodents, monkeys, wildebeest and birds. Leopards mainly hunt at night. They will attempt to get as close as possible to their prey without alerting them, and in the right moment they will strike from their hiding place. Their prey is then killed via suffocation.

Fun Facts

  • Leopards normally store their kills in trees. This prevents other animals such as lions or hyenas from stealing their prey
  • Leopards can also be black. These are known as melanistic leopards
  • Leopards can see 7 times better in the dark than people
